Tips for Getting the Best Word to PDF Conversion

A few small things can make your converted PDF much cleaner and more professional.

Use Standard Fonts in Your Word File

Use common fonts like Arial, Times New Roman, or Calibri in your Word document. Unusual or decorative fonts may not convert correctly to PDF. Stick to standard fonts to ensure the output looks exactly as intended.

Set Correct Page Margins Before Converting

Incorrect margins in your Word file will carry over to the PDF. Before converting, check that your margins are set correctly — typically 1 inch on all sides is the standard. This ensures your content is not cut off at the edges in the final PDF.

Remove Tracked Changes and Comments

If your Word document has tracked changes or review comments enabled, accept or reject all changes before converting. Otherwise those edits may appear in the PDF output. Go to Review in Word and accept all changes before saving the file.
