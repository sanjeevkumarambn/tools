Understanding Age Relaxation in Government Jobs

Age relaxation is an additional allowance given to candidates from certain categories. It effectively raises the upper age limit for those candidates. Understanding how it works helps you check your eligibility more accurately.

Standard Age Relaxation for Central Government Jobs

Here is the standard age relaxation for most central government recruitment boards. State government notifications may differ — always check the specific notification.

  • OBC (Non-Creamy Layer): 3 years relaxation above the upper age limit
  • SC / ST candidates: 5 years relaxation above the upper age limit
  • PwD (General category): 10 years relaxation above the upper age limit
  • PwD (OBC category): 13 years relaxation above the upper age limit
  • PwD (SC/ST category): 15 years relaxation above the upper age limit
  • Ex-Servicemen: Service period plus 3 additional years

How to Apply Relaxation in Your Age Check

Say a notification sets the upper age limit at 27 years for General category. You are an OBC candidate and your calculated age on the cut-off date is 28 years and 4 months. You might assume you are not eligible. But OBC candidates get 3 years of relaxation — so your effective upper limit is 30 years. You are fully eligible. Always calculate your base age first, then apply the category relaxation before concluding eligibility.

Age Relaxation for Government Employees

Central government employees who are already in service may receive additional age relaxation of 5 years for certain posts. This varies by notification. If you are already working in a central government department, check whether this relaxation applies to the specific vacancy you are applying for.
