What Does Splitting a PDF Mean?

Splitting a PDF means breaking one large PDF file into smaller parts. You can split it into individual pages, or extract only a specific range of pages. The original file remains unchanged. You get a new PDF with only the pages you selected.

This is different from compressing a PDF. Compressing a PDF reduces the file size. Splitting separates the pages into a smaller file. You may sometimes need to do both — split first, then compress — especially for government portal submissions.

Split vs Extract — What is the Difference?

People often confuse splitting and extracting. Splitting divides the full PDF into multiple parts at once. Extracting means pulling out specific pages and saving them as a separate PDF. The Rojgar Sangam Tools Split PDF tool supports both methods — you choose the pages you want and download only those.
