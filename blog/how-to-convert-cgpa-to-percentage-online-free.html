What Is CGPA and Why Government Forms Ask for Percentage

CGPA stands for Cumulative Grade Point Average. It is the grading system used by most Indian universities and colleges. Marks are converted into grade points, and the average of all semesters gives you your CGPA on a scale of 10 (or 4 in some systems).

Why Government Forms Still Ask for Percentage

Government recruitment forms in India were designed when percentage was the standard way of reporting academic performance. Most portals and application systems still use percentage as the field type. They have not been updated to accept CGPA directly. This means every student from a CGPA-based university must convert their score before applying.

The Risk of Entering a Wrong Percentage

If you enter an incorrect percentage — too high or too low — it can create problems at the document verification stage. If your percentage on the form does not match what is verifiable from your marksheet, the verification officer may flag your application. Always convert using the formula specified by your university, not a random online result.

Which Recruitment Portals Ask for Percentage

Almost all major Indian recruitment portals ask for percentage:

  • SSC CGL, CHSL, MTS — percentage field in educational qualification section
  • IBPS PO, Clerk, SO — graduation percentage required for eligibility
  • Railway NTPC and Group D — percentage on marksheet or equivalent
  • State government posts — percentage in the relevant degree
  • PSU recruitment forms — minimum percentage cutoffs for shortlisting

CGPA to Percentage Formula — Complete Guide

There is no single universal formula for all universities. But the most commonly used method in India is the CGPA to percentage formula based on a multiplier applied to your CGPA.

The Standard CBSE Formula — Multiply by 9.5

For students on a 10-point grading scale, the most widely accepted formula is:

Percentage = CGPA × 9.5

This formula is recommended by CBSE and is used as the default by many Indian universities and recruitment portals. Here are some examples using this formula:

  • 6.0 CGPA = 6.0 × 9.5 = 57%
  • 7.0 CGPA = 7.0 × 9.5 = 66.5%
  • 7.5 CGPA = 7.5 × 9.5 = 71.25%
  • 8.0 CGPA = 8.0 × 9.5 = 76%
  • 8.5 CGPA = 8.5 × 9.5 = 80.75%
  • 9.0 CGPA = 9.0 × 9.5 = 85.5%

Other University-Specific Multipliers

Not every university uses the 9.5 multiplier. Here are common variations:

  • Multiply by 10: Used by some universities that directly equate 1 grade point to 10%
  • Multiply by 9.0: Used by certain state universities and deemed universities
  • Institution-specific formula: Some universities have a custom chart printed on the marksheet itself

Always check your university's official website or look at your marksheet for the stated conversion formula before using any calculator.

CGPA on a 4-Point Scale — US-Style Grading

Some private universities and IITs use a 4-point or 4.33-point grading system. Converting a 4-point CGPA to percentage is different. One common method is:

Percentage = (CGPA / 4) × 100

So a CGPA of 3.5 on a 4-point scale gives (3.5 / 4) × 100 = 87.5%. The CGPA to Percentage Converter tool on Rojgar Sangam Tools supports both 10-point and 4-point scales.

What Percentage to Enter on Government Job Application Forms

This is the most important practical question. Here is how to handle it correctly.

When Your University Specifies a Conversion Formula

If your marksheet or your university's website clearly states the conversion formula — for example, "multiply CGPA by 9.5" — use that formula and enter the resulting percentage. This is the most defensible value during document verification.

When Your University Does Not Specify Any Formula

If your university does not provide an official conversion formula, use the standard CBSE formula: CGPA × 9.5. This is the most widely accepted default in India for 10-point scale grades. Most recruitment boards and verification officers are familiar with this formula.

When the Form Asks for "Percentage of Marks Obtained"

Some forms use this exact phrase. This is simply asking for your percentage — the converted value from your CGPA. Enter the converted percentage directly. Do not write your CGPA value in this field. If you have a percentage-based marksheet from your Class 12 or a previous degree, enter that percentage directly without conversion.
