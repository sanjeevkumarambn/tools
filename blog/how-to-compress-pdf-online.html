What is PDF Compression and Why Does It Matter?

PDF compression is the process of reducing the file size of a PDF document while keeping its content — text, images, and formatting — as intact as possible. A compressed PDF takes less storage space and uploads faster on slow internet connections, which is especially useful in rural areas where internet speed can be limited.

For government job applications, compressed PDFs are not just convenient — they are often mandatory. Portals enforce file size limits to keep their servers efficient and to ensure all applicants submit standardised documents.

Common Reasons Why Your PDF File Size Is Too Large

  • You scanned a physical document at very high resolution (300 DPI or above)
  • Your PDF contains multiple high-quality images embedded inside
  • The document was exported from Word or another software with embedded fonts
  • The file contains unnecessary metadata, bookmarks, or hidden layers
  • You merged multiple PDFs without compressing them first
Important Warning

Do not compress a PDF more than necessary. Over-compression makes text blurry and unreadable, which may cause your application to be rejected during document verification by the recruiting authority.

Which Compression Level Should You Choose?

Low Compression — Best Quality

Use Low compression when your PDF is already fairly small (under 1MB) or when the document contains important text and fine print that must remain sharp. This setting reduces file size by roughly 20 to 40 percent while preserving maximum quality.

Medium Compression — Best Balance

Medium compression is the recommended setting for most government job documents. It reduces file size by 50 to 70 percent while keeping text fully readable. This is the right choice for scanned marksheets, certificates, and identity documents.

High Compression — Smallest File Size

Use High compression only when you absolutely need to hit a very low file size limit (under 100KB) and your document does not contain very fine text or critical details. Images inside the PDF may appear slightly less sharp at this setting.

Recommended Settings for Different Portals

SSC and UPSC Applications

Most SSC and UPSC portals require documents under 200KB to 300KB. Use Medium compression for scanned certificates and Low compression for digitally created PDFs.

Railway Recruitment Board (RRB)

Railway portals typically accept files up to 500KB. Low compression is usually sufficient and will preserve better document quality for verification.

State PSC and Police Recruitment

Requirements vary by state. Most state portals accept files between 100KB and 500KB. Always read the official notification carefully for exact specifications before compressing.

Bank and IBPS Applications

Bank portals such as IBPS, SBI, and RBI usually allow up to 300KB per document. Medium compression works best for the combination of text and scanned images found in bank application documents.
