JPG vs PDF — What is the Difference?

A JPG file is a compressed image file. It can be resized, cropped, and edited easily. A PDF file is a fixed-layout document. It looks the same on every device. Government portals prefer PDF because it is stable, secure, and cannot be accidentally edited. Converting your JPEG to PDF gives your document a professional, submission-ready format.

Tips for Taking Good Photos Before Converting to PDF

A good JPG photo produces a good PDF. These tips will help you take clear, submission-ready photos of your documents.

Use Good Lighting

Place your document near a window in natural light. Avoid taking photos under yellow tube lights — they cause uneven shadows on the document. Natural daylight produces the clearest and most readable photos. Avoid using flash as it creates glare on the paper.

Keep the Camera Straight

Hold your phone directly above the document. Do not tilt or angle the camera. A straight, top-down shot ensures all four corners of the document are visible and the text is not distorted. If your phone has a document scanner mode, use it for even better results.

Check the Image Before Converting

After taking the photo, zoom in and read the text. Make sure all words are sharp and legible. Check that the document is not cut off at any edge. If anything looks unclear, take the photo again before converting it to PDF.
