Did You Know

Many government recruitment boards — including several state PSC boards and examination authorities — ask candidates to submit photos with their name and date of birth printed in block letters below the photo. This requirement appears in the application instructions, not always on the form itself. Read the full notification carefully before preparing your photo.

Why Government Portals Ask You to Add Name and DOB on Photo

The name and date of birth requirement exists for a practical reason. During large-scale government recruitment drives, thousands of admit cards and application sheets are printed and handled manually. When a photo has the candidate's name and date of birth printed on it, officials can identify the correct person quickly without checking multiple documents.

Portals and Forms That Commonly Require This

Here are the common situations where you will need a photo with name and DOB printed on it:

  • Self-attestation sheets submitted along with application printouts
  • State government recruitment portals that specify this in the photo upload instructions
  • Offline application forms for state police, teacher, and clerical recruitment
  • Admit cards where the candidate must paste a photo and handwrite their name below it
  • Document verification rounds where a labelled photo is required as additional ID

Printed vs Handwritten — What Is Acceptable

Most recruitment notifications accept both printed and handwritten text on the photo. However, printed text is always neater and more legible. When you use the Name DOB on Photo tool, the text is printed digitally in a clean, uniform font. This looks professional and avoids the risk of untidy handwriting causing issues during verification.

What Happens If You Skip This Step

If the notification specifically asks for name and date of birth on the photo and you submit a plain photo without it, your application can be rejected during document verification. In offline submissions, the verification officer may ask you to resubmit with a correctly labelled photo. In online portals, the uploaded file may be flagged. Always read the photo upload instructions carefully before submitting.

How to Add Name and Date of Birth on Photo Online Free — Getting the Details Right

The content you add matters as much as the process. Here is what to check before finalising your photo.

Name Format — Use Your Full Legal Name

Always use your full name exactly as it appears in your Class 10 or Matriculation certificate. Do not use a short form, nickname, or initials unless the notification specifically allows it. For example, if your certificate says "RAJESH KUMAR SHARMA", do not write "Rajesh Sharma" on the photo. Even a small difference can cause a mismatch during document verification.

Date of Birth Format — Match the Notification

Different portals specify different date formats. The most common formats are:

  • DD/MM/YYYY — example: 15/06/2000
  • DD-MM-YYYY — example: 15-06-2000
  • DD Month YYYY — example: 15 June 2000

Check the photo upload instructions in the notification carefully. Use the format they specify. If no format is mentioned, use DD/MM/YYYY as the standard.

Text Colour — Black Is Always Safe

Always use black text on your photo. It is the most readable colour and is universally accepted across all government portals and offline forms. Avoid red, blue, or any other colour unless explicitly instructed by the notification.
